Deepti Sudhindra began her bharatanatyam training under the guidance of Guru Padmini Ravi in 1984.  She performed with the Pradhan Dance Centre for a decade, before embarking on a career in English Theatre.

She co founded the Kimaaya Theatre Company, where she produced, directed and acted in some path breaking theatre work. A keen interest in the performing arts, Deepti has lent her voice and design skills to many productions and is also the creative director of The Jewelry Project, a design firm working in metal crafts.

She was shortlisted for the young creative entrepreneur awards for fashion, by the British Council in 2011 for her work in creating a new grammar of ornament in Indian jewelry.

Two years ago, Deepti began her training with Padmini Ravi, to bring back into her life, a deeper understanding of tradition and roots, and to explore the notions of free movement in a classical format.

Today bringing together twenty years of experience in the performing arts, she presents a piece based on the grammar of bharatanatyam and theatre.
