Madras String Quartet:
 Madras String Quartet (MSQ) founded in 1993, based in Chennai, South India performed originally regular western music repertoire.  The founder and first violinist of the quartet, V.S. Narasimhan, who had a formidable base in Carnatic music (southern Indian classical system) embarked on a remarkable musical voyage, exploring new frontiers in chamber music performance with his quartet with a unique combination of Southern Indian classical music with western harmonic principles.

 

This original integration of the Indian and Western classical music systems has been widely hailed as ground breaking work; unlike many other fusion works that exist, the original compositions of the great composers of South India are rendered in the utmost pure form.

 

MSQ’s album "Resonance", and the albums "RagaSaga" and "Seamless Strings" are full of Narasimhan’s delicate and intricate arrangements replete with an enduring blend of Western harmonies, counterpoint, and pulsating rhythmic structures.


Members of the Madras String Quartet:
V.S.Narasimhan (1st Violin) comes from a musical family.  His father Gottuvadyam V. Srinivasa Iyengar initiated him into the South Indian classical music when he was five and has accompanied his father’s Gottuvadyam concerts in many places in Karnataka. As a young boy Narasimhan had a special interest in western classical music as he started working in the South Indian Film Industry. He studied western violin with Mr. Adrian L’Armand of Australia.

 

Hemanthraj Muliyil (2nd Violin) was interested in western classical music even from his childhood. He had his early training from a violin-teacher in his hometown Calicut. With his association with Lancelot Thomas, a conductor & pianist of the Calicut Musical Association, and later with the Madras Philharmonic & Choral Society founded by Handel Manuel, helped Hemanthraj to shape up his artistic ability. Hemanthraj is also specially skilled in the repairing of the instruments.

 

B.J.Chandran, (Viola) coming from a family of musicians from Andhra Pradesh, was initiated into South Indian Classical music. Later he switched over to western violin by studying with John Taylor in Madras. Chandran is a very talented and sensitive young violinist, who took to playing viola especially for the quartet.

 

V.R.Sekar (Cello) is the son of the most popular Violin Vidwan / Music Director Kunnakkudi R. Vaidyanathan.  Sekar started learning Carnatic Music on the violin as a young boy. Fascinated by its sound, he took Cello as his main instrument and now is the most sought after young cellist of India.
