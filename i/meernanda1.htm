Dr. (Mrs.) MEERNANDA BARTHAKUR is a noted classical dancer of Assam, most popular for her unique style of presentation on the stage. From her early childhood she has been performing classical dance recitals continuously all over Assam as well as in different parts of the country. During her school days Meernanda won medals in several Dance competitions of Assam. She is an ‘A’ Grade Sattriya artist of Doordarshan, New-Delhi. She is the proud recepient of the Ustad Bismillah Khan Yuva Puraskar (2008) by the Sangeet Natak Akademi, New Delhi, (the highest award in India for under 35years) for her oustanding talent and contribution in the field of Sattriya. She received the 4th. Devdasi National Award from the Devdasi Nrutya Mandir, Bhubaneswar.

 

Meernanda started her early lessons in Sattriya Dance in Sangeet Sattra at a tender age of 8yrs, under Guru Gobinda Saikia. Later she stepped into Pushpanjali Cultural Academy of Guwahati and received training and performed Sattriya dance under Guru Pushpa Bhuyan (Padmashree awardee) and Barbayan Tankeswar Hazarika. Since 1999 she has entirely devoted herself to Sattriya Dance under her Guru Nrityacharyya Shri Jatin Goswami (Padmashree & Sangeet Natak Akademi awardee) .She has also trained in Ojapaali under Sangeet Natak Akademi awardee Manik Bora Barbayan.

 

Dr.Meernanda has number of treatises and articles to her credit. Among many lecture-demonstrations in Sattriya, the most noteworthy is her presentation at  the Nritya Sangam, Kolkata and Nritya Parva Guwahati; which earned her bountiful appreciation of the experts and critics alike. She has worked (2006 -’08) as a fellow under the Ministry of Tourism and Culture, Govt. of India, on ‘Evolving a repertoire of Sattriya dance for procenium stage’.

          Dr. Meernanda devotes and committed to Sattriya Dance and its development to the fullest form. So she makes frequent visits to the different Sattras of Assam in her academic pursuit, to observe the Sattriya Dances in their original forms, running for hundreds of years in those Sattras.

          As the director of “NRITYA KALPA”, an organization for training, development and research oriented works of the Sattriya dance form, Dr. Meernanda has dedicated her self to prepare the next generation for this beautiful living tradition.
