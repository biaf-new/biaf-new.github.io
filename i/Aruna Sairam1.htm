Padma Shri Aruna Sairam, regarded as a Music Ambassador of India, has successfully taken Indian music to the global arena. Her style is rooted in tradition, yet continuesto evolve  in the contemporary. Her voice, a beautiful blend of resonance and range, has an unusual timbre which gives it uniqueness. She has created transformational music and through her repertoire of South Indian Classical (Carnatic) music dazzled audiences at prestigious musical venues around the world including The Royal Albert Hall in London, Carnegie Hall in New York, Le Theatre de la Ville in Paris, Opera National in Lyons, Royce Hall in Los Angeles, The Festival of World Sacred Music in Morocco, The Music Academy in Chennai and Chowdiah Hall in Bengaluru. She has had the rare distinction of performing at the Indian President's official residence - Rashtrapati Bhavan, at the memorials of former Prime Ministers, Indira Gandhi and Rajiv Gandhi,Shakthi Sthal and Vir Bhoomi, and at the Commonwealth Games, New Delhi.

Smt. Aruna began her classical training under her mother Smt. RajalakshmiSethuraman.Aruna belongs to VeenaiDhanammalschool of Carnatic music, known for its strict adherence to tradition and form, and is a disciple of the legendary T. Brinda. Her extensive training, depth of musical knowledge and innate musicality allowAruna the freedom to explore and innovate. Under the guidance of A.S. Mani, disciple of the legendary Tiger Varadachariar,  Smt. Aruna developed a unique  command over swarakalpanas.  VeenaVidwanSri.K.S. Narayanaswamy and Sri. T.R. Subramaniam helped her gain mastery over the ragas with an insightful understanding of the Pallavi.

Arunais a trendsetter. She was the first to introduce the Abhang, a distinctive folk music from western India, into a very traditional Southern Indian Carnatic concert. She has collaborated with leading European musician sand presented Carnatic music with Classical Liturgical, Medieval and Gregorian Chants, Arabo-Andulasian, Flamenco and Neo Classical Music.

Aruna’s panache for innovation has brought her accolades as a trail-blazer in Carnatic music. Among the many national and international honors that have been bestowed upon Arunaare thePadma Shri, one of the highest honors awarded by the Indian Government to civilians of merit, and the prestigious US Congress Proclamation of Excellence, wherein the U.S. national flag was flown atop the Capitol building in her honor.

Smt. Aruna believes in  the guru-shishyaparamapara, which creates a one-on-one environment between teacher and student.  She has undertaken various mentoring programmes including the recent BBC World Routes Academy Project - where she a groomed a British born, South Asian Veena player, HariSivanesan. She taught Carnatic Classical Music to a French singer of Medieval chant, Marco Horvat,  under the Indo-French Cultural Exchange Programme.  She has also taught at ‘Harvard Sangeet’ – for students of Harvard University, USA.  Smt. Aruna was appointed by the Chief Minister of Tamil Nadu as Advisor to the Department of Culture, Tamil Nadu on Musical Education between 2007 and 2011.

Aruna’s vision is to preserve the art of South Indian Classical music for future generations. She is contributing towards this goal by inculcating the next generation into this form and by supporting all aspects of the arts’ ecosystem including the  performing and accompanying artists, the instrument makers, listeners, organizers, senior musicians,  gurus, critics and press.

The Aruna Sairam Foundation fosters every aspect of this ecosystem by giving performance opportunities to young musicians,  facilitating a flow of knowledge from senior gurus to young students, encouraging dialogue between different groups that are engaged in music, and taking classical music to under privileged children. Further, Smt. Aruna’sNadayogam Trust is dedicated to in-depth research in Music.  Her  library consists of approximately 5000 books and rare records. The trust is currently devoted to archiving these records to make it accessible for public use.

Smt. Aruna has donated proceeds from her concerts and sale of her records to cancer hospitals and to rehabilitate families of musicians who have passed away during their professional peak. She has performed for underprivileged children in orphanages in India and Malaysia.
