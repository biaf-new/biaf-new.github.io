Dancer/Performer/Choreographer/Teacher

 

First Steps
I was born and raised in Poland. When I was 7 I started to learn ballroom dance but after 6 years of cha-cha, jive and waltz, my interest shifted...

Early Search
As a teenager I became fascinated with alternative theatre, but my passion towards movement was too strong to be ignored. I thought to myself, there must be something which combines all of this together. This is how my adventure with dance and physical theatre began...

Direction
My interests and passions within life drifted into the realm of contemporary dance, physical theatre and contact improvisation.
Immersed in a world of new discoveries, opportunities, and possibilities, my thirst led me onto the path of performing, choreographing and teaching.

Inspiration
I am deeply interested in human behaviour.
I think people are amazing - we are amazing, we are so colourful and beautifully complex beings.
I am impressed by the composite of our character - we are an endless treasure chest of mysterious inspiration...

Education
In 2007 I graduated from the University of Zielona Gora (Poland) with a master's degree in Pedagogy from the Faculty of Education and Social Sciences  [Specialisation: Socio-Cultural Animation in Dance].

Iceland
After finishing my studies I packed my suitcase and went to visit Iceland.
During 4 years in Iceland I have been  sharing my knowledge, creating, and enjoying this unforgettable land.

India

In 2011 I decided to fulfill a childhood dream and I went to India - Shivas colorful country - where I was shareing knowledge with local dancers and students and getting closer to the spirit...
​

​Performer

In 2004, during XII International Presentation of Contemporary Dance Forms (Kalisz, Poland), Kama performed as part of an independent project, 'Wycinanka' (Cut-Out).
The group won 1st Prize in 'Creating Realism on Stage' and individually Kama received a prize for 'Stage Presence'.

Following this festival, Kama was approached by choreographers HelgeLetonja and GüntherGrollitsch from Steptext Dance Company (Bremen, Germany) to work in their dance projects. It was through this experience that Kama realised dance was no longer going to be her hobby; it would become her profession.

Kama is now a member of The Icelandic Association of Professional Dancers.
She is travelling; always creating, performing and exploring new [choreographic] ideas...

Choreographer

This is what excites me...

Her early fascination with alternative forms of theatre strongly influences her work and permeates through her craft.
As a choreographer Kama is working with dancers, actors, and students.

Teacher

Kama specialises in teaching contemporary dance, choreography, improvisation, and European performance practices. She also teaches philosophy of a movement & body awarness in Tango, and she leads one-on-one or group sessions in which the dynamic forces of movement and breath are combined with meditation.She was a head of Contemporary Dance department in Reykjavik Dance Academy.
